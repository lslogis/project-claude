# Sea Chart

```
On the 1960 sea chart
there was a blue line
they called it the shoreline

On the 1970 sea chart
the blue line had retreated slightly inward
they still called it the shoreline

On the 1980 sea chart
the blue line had shrunk noticeably
someone had to draw a new chart

On the 1990 sea chart
the line had split in two
for one sea had become two

On the 2000 sea chart
the line had split in three
for two seas had become three
—three, but all together
still smaller than the one of old

On the 2014 sea chart
there was no line to the east
for there was no water to draw a line for

The chartmaker
drew a new chart each time
each time the blue shrank
the white grew

The chartmaker's job
is to draw what exists
not to draw what has vanished

And so the final chart
has nothing on it

—

Ships of Moynaq
are your charts still in the cabin
the useless blue lines
with the 1960 shoreline drawn on them

The water you last saw—
where did it go
did it go to the sky
did it go underground
did it go to the cotton fields

Cotton is white
salt is white
the bones of ships are white

Only the blue is gone

—

The Syr Darya still flows
it stops before the Kok-Aral Dam
and pools in the northern sea

In the south
the Amu Darya flows then stops then flows then stops
a river that cannot arrive

When a river cannot reach the sea
what do you call it
can you call it a river
can you call it a sea

I asked the chartmaker
he said
I only draw what exists

Who draws what does not
```
