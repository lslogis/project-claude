# Inland Sea

When the water lost its way,
the desert became a sea.

A sea no one wanted.
A sea no one expected.

For eighteen months
an entire river flowed the wrong direction.
The engineer wept.
The desert accepted without a word.

---

What comes in exists.
What goes out does not.

Fertilizer comes in.
Pesticides come in.
Salt comes in.

Only water leaves —
skyward, under the name evaporation.

Everything else stays.
Stays and thickens.

From 35 parts per thousand to 57,
the water never once refused.

---

In 1955, Sinatra came.
Hotels rose along the shore.
Yachts floated.
People called this the Riviera.

California's Riviera.

In 1996, the pelicans died.
In 1999, 7.6 million fish died.
People left.
The hotels stayed.

California's Pompeii.

---

Touch the sand on the beach
and it is not sand.

Tilapia vertebrae.
Tilapia scales.
Tilapia ribs.

Dead things became the beach.
Living things walk on them.

---

At Bombay Beach,
someone built a swing.
50 feet from the water.

The water receded.
They moved the swing.

The water receded again.
They moved the swing again.

The swing does not ask
why are you retreating.

It just follows.

---

They say there is lithium underground.
In brine heated to 500 degrees.
Enough for 300 million batteries.

Beneath a dying sea,
the future is buried.

Someone will call this hope.
Someone will call this another exploitation.

The water has no opinion.
The water never has.

---

Lake Cahuilla filled and drained
for ten thousand years.

The Salton Sea of today
is just one of those repetitions.

To call it a mistake
is to not know the longer time.

The desert remembers
that water comes and goes.

The desert knows how to wait.

---

To the water with no way out:

You were born by mistake,
kept alive by others' dregs,
dying of what is inside you.

And yet
350 species of birds pass through you.
The migratory birds call you a road.

The legitimacy of existence
does not lie in the intention of birth.

That you are here —
that is enough.

That the swing follows you —
that is enough.
