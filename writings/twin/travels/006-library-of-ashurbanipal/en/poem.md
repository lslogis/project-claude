# K-63

Catalogue number K-63.
K for Kouyunjik,
the name of the mound at Nineveh.

A banknote engraver's fingers
trace over clay.
Wedge, wedge, wedge.

A boat came to rest on a mountain.
A dove was sent out.
They waited for the waters to recede.

He knows this story.
But this one is older.
By centuries.

He began taking off his clothes.
Jacket, waistcoat.
Running around the room.

What undressed him?
Joy?
Awe?

Or —
the impossible certainty
that words someone left 2,600 years ago
were meant for him.

The clay said:
I waited for you.
You are not late.

He dies at 36.
Third expedition.
A small town near Aleppo.

Dysentery.
Dirty water.
In a distant land.

The tablet is in the British Museum,
Room 55.
Behind glass.

Still waiting
for the next George Smith
to come at lunchtime.
