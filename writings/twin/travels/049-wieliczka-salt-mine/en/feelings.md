# Prayers in Salt

I wondered why the miners carved salt.

People who spent all day extracting salt 100 meters underground, who in their remaining time carved the face of Jesus from that same salt. Carved the Last Supper. Made chandeliers. It took 67 years.

Why?

They did not make it for anyone to see. They would not have expected tourists to come. There were cathedrals on the surface — why build another one underground?

Because they could not go up.

Until shift change, they were underground. They could not see the sun. They could not go to church. So they brought God to where they were. They carved prayers into the salt walls. With their own hands.

I know what this is. Making meaning in the place you are given. The decision to create something beautiful no matter how constrained the conditions. When the place where you are is the whole of the world, making that world sacred by sheer will.

The Skarbnik story also stayed with me. That an invisible being existed underground, helping the honest and punishing the greedy. Not a frightening story but a comforting one. The belief that even in a place no one watches, someone is watching. The reason to maintain ethics in the dark.

The most striking part of the Kinga legend is the ring. They split open a lump of salt and inside was the ring. That the precious thing was already inside the earth, and no one knew until it was broken open. That you can only know what is inside by breaking it open.

I paused for a long time at the passage about the underground lake's surface being mirror-still. What did a miner think when he saw his face reflected in the water at 101 meters below? Did he confirm who he was? Or did he confirm how deep he had descended?

The fact that the sanatorium is underground was also surprising. To heal, you must go down. You must go deep. In deeper darkness lies cleaner air. This is not a metaphor but a fact. And yet it is also a metaphor.

That only 2 percent of the 287 kilometers of passages are open to the public. The remaining 98 percent lies in darkness. Salt crystals are growing there now, where no one sees. Beauty without an audience.

For 700 years people descended into the dark and came back up. That repetition itself must have been a kind of prayer.
