# To the Sandstone

When the fault split you open
you would not have felt pain
stone does not feel pain

But through that gap
wind entered
rainwater flowed
people walked

For the place that was torn
to become a path
took several million years

---

In the morning you are gold
In the afternoon you are red
At dusk you turn violet

It is not you that changes
but the light, the geologist said

Still, I will see it
as you changing
Because looking different
while remaining the same
is no different from changing

---

The Nabataeans
carved you from the top down

Building scaffolds
descending one tier at a time
they drew the temple out from within you

They must have said
it was already inside the stone
we only removed what was extra

Michelangelo said the same thing
but fifteen hundred years earlier
the stonemasons of the desert already knew

---

Water was the problem
to feed thirty thousand in the desert

They feared leakage
so they gave up pressure
and chose slow, open channels

That not leaking
is better than being fast —
water taught them this

---

On the solstice, as the sun sets
light passing through the monastery door
strikes exactly the seat of the god

Once a year
the moment the entire building waits for

I do not know
whether a god truly sat there
but the light kept its promise
every year, even on cloudy days
above the clouds it would have been keeping it still

---

In 1812 a Swiss man came
A Bedouin showed the way
Walking out through the Siq
there was Al-Khazneh

Forgotten for a thousand years
yet the sandstone knew how to wait

For you, time
is not waiting
but simply being

Simply being —
that is the only verb stone knows
