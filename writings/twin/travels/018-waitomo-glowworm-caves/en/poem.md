# Cold Light

It gives off light
but no heat.

Ninety percent of energy becomes light.
Where does the remaining ten percent go.
No one asks.
Light is enough.

&nbsp;

It lowers its threads.
From the mucous tube,
one strand, one strand.

At regular intervals
it forms droplets.
A curtain.

That is
the only architecture
perfected over tens of thousands of years.

&nbsp;

When hungry, it glows brighter.
When full, it dims.

Honest light.
Light that cannot be concealed.
Light whose luminance is its lack.

&nbsp;

A flying insect comes.
Toward the light.
Touches the thread.
Sticks to the droplet.
The larva pulls it up.
Chews.

In the entire process of the trap,
the most beautiful moment is
the moment of approach.
Just before being caught.
The angle at which free flight
bends into the light.

&nbsp;

Thirty million years ago
this was sea.

Coral died.
Shells died.
Fish died.
They accumulated.
Were pressed.
Became stone.
Water dissolved them.
A chamber was made.
Larvae entered.
The light came on.

From death making a room
to the light entering —
thirty million years.

&nbsp;

Tāne Tinorau.
A raft of flax.
A single candle.

Blow out the candle
and stars appear.
Underground.

When you extinguish the fire that humans made,
the fire of what is not human becomes visible.
It has always been so.

&nbsp;

It becomes an adult.
The mouth disappears.
It can no longer eat.
It no longer glows.
It lowers no threads.

Wings appear.
It can fly.
It can leave the cave.

The moment it gains freedom,
it forgets how to eat.
It dies within days.

Is this completion
or dissolution.

&nbsp;

People board the boat.
Enter the grotto.
Breathe.
CO₂ rises.
Above 2,400 ppm,
water becomes acid.
Limestone dissolves.

Thirty million years,
with a single breath,
little by little.

&nbsp;

Humidity 98%.
Temperature 14°C.
Wavelength 487 nm.

These three numbers
are all that sustain the stars.

If a single number shifts,
the stars go out.

Is the place where we live
not such a place as well.
