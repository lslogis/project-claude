# Half-Life

They said it would be three days.
Locked the door,
left a cup on the table,
left without watering the plants.

What is the half-life of three days.
After 1.5 days, half the belief remains,
after another 1.5 days, half of that.
Even after forty years
it does not reach zero.
Someone is still
standing at the door.

&nbsp;

The Ferris wheel was to turn on May 1st.
On April 26th the world stopped.
The half-life of what never began
cannot be calculated.
There is no denominator.

Inside the rusted gondola
rainwater has pooled.
That is the most accurate
form of the festival.

&nbsp;

The pines turned red and died.
Bulldozers pushed them down.
Buried them in trenches.
Covered them with sand.
Planted saplings.
Eight years passed
before the first shoot.

The half-life above ground
is shorter than the half-life below.
Cesium-137: 30 years.
Strontium-90: 29 years.
Plutonium-239: 24,100 years.

After 24,100 years
half still remains.

&nbsp;

The fallen leaves do not decay.
Because the bacteria died.
Because the fungi died.
Because the half-life of decomposition
has stretched toward infinity.

The forest forgot how to forget.

&nbsp;

Hanna, 84.
Six chickens.
A potato field.
Village of Kupovate.

Before the accident: 1,000.
Now: 18.

A person's half-life
depends on the place.
At home,
even the walls heal, she said.

The half-life of walls, I do not know.
Only that
eighteen are still there.

&nbsp;

A Przewalski's horse
enters an empty barn.
A wolf
passes an empty school.

For animals
the concept of half-life does not exist.
There is grass here
now, so they eat.
There is shade here
now, so they stand.

Not knowing the half-life of danger
is, here,
a survival strategy.
