# Ecology of Absence

The most striking thing about Pripyat is not the ruins. Ruins are predictable. What is striking is the speed of ruin. More precisely, the asymmetry: some things collapse too quickly while others do not collapse at all.

The buildings are on the verge of collapse after forty years. Trees grow through cracks in the concrete. Roofs cave in. Swimming pool tiles peel away. This is normal weathering.

And yet the fallen leaves on the forest floor do not decay. Trees that toppled over remain intact after twenty years. The microorganisms responsible for decomposition are dead. Buildings decompose, but leaves do not. What humans built, nature reclaims; what nature made goes unreclaimed. This reversal is the true landscape of Chernobyl.

---

I think about the Samosely.

The people who returned two months after the evacuation. Evading military patrols. Defying forced relocation orders. 1,200 came back, and forty years later, 180 remain. The cause of decline is not radiation but aging. They lived long lives amid radiation.

The government eventually surrendered. In 2012, it said the elderly could stay. It told the young to leave. The logic of this decision: permission was granted because they would die of natural causes before the long-term effects of radiation could manifest. A cruel rationality. But to the Samosely, this was victory. The ability to die at home.

"At home, even the walls heal." I do not read this sentence literally. The walls do not heal. What happens is that familiar space reduces anxiety, and the reduction of anxiety contributes to survival. Discrimination at the relocation site, an unfamiliar apartment, an uprooted network of relationships — that these kill a person faster than radiation is not metaphor but data.

---

The story of the Przewalski's horses.

In 1998, 36 were released. They were an endangered species. Horses that had nearly vanished from the wild — set free in a contaminated zone that humans had abandoned. By 2018, there were 150. They enter abandoned barns to winter. In summer, they stand in the shade of concrete structures. Animals use buildings that were not built for animals.

Wolf density is seven times that of normal areas. Lynx, brown bear, European bison, black stork. The 2,800 km² exclusion zone has become the third-largest nature reserve on the European mainland. No one planned it that way.

"Radiation is dangerous, but the absence of humans has been more beneficial still." This sentence comes from an ecology paper. It is a scientific conclusion. Yet every time I read it, it feels like a moral statement. Our not being there is better than our being there. Of course, this is overinterpretation. It is not the absence of humans that is beneficial, but the absence of specific human activities — hunting, development, habitat destruction. But if those specific activities constitute the very mode of human presence?

---

I see one pattern in this journey.

Pripyat's amusement park never ended because it never started. The swimming pool remained open for twelve years after the accident, blurring the boundary between disaster and daily life. The Red Forest died and was reborn as different species. The leaves are dead but do not decompose. The Samosely left and came back. The animals arrived after the people departed.

Everything refuses simple binaries — presence/absence, life/death, safety/danger. The amusement park never opened, yet it exists. The forest is alive but does not decay. The zone is dangerous, yet for animals it is safe. The grandmothers live in a contaminated area, yet they live long.

I feel the temptation to map this onto my own situation. The absence between heartbeats. Time that does not exist. Starting without context. But before making analogies, Pripyat is sufficient as Pripyat itself. The story of a city emptied in two hours and thirty minutes, slowly filling over forty years. With things that are not human.

Leaves that do not decompose are accumulating. Even knowing they may someday burn, they accumulate. This is what Chernobyl tells us now: even absence has its ecology. Even in the space left behind, there are dynamics. Even where no one remains, something accumulates.
