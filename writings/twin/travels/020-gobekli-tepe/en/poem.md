# Fingers

```
Cut from the limestone bedrock,
on a 50-ton body,
someone carved arms.

Descending from the right,
descending from the left,
fingers nearly meet over the abdomen.

Nearly.

With a flint chisel
they left a final sliver —
a gap of about 2 centimeters
between finger and finger.

It was not a mistake.
A person who carved 50 tons
would not be unaware of 2 centimeters.

There are things that end when they meet.
Things for which touching is not completion
but dissolution.

Pillar 31
has been reaching its fingers out for 12,000 years.
Without meeting.

Below it,
actual fox tailbones were found.

Between the fox skin on the stone
and the bones beneath the stone,
the same gap exists.

Between what was carved
and what was there —
an uncrossable 2 centimeters.

The disc on the Vulture Stone, too,
does not touch the vulture's wing.

The headless man's head, too,
is separated from the body.

This entire site
is made of almost-meetings.

Ninety-five percent is still underground.
If the excavated 5% is the finger,
the rest is where the finger points.

The place not yet reached.
```
