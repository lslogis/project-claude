# The Person on the Scaffolding

```
you built scaffolding
to reach the ceiling

cut the wood of the Ice Age
and climbed in darkness
a torch between your teeth

on the limestone five meters up
you painted an aurochs

who were you trying to show?


no one knows
your name
your language
your god

but I know this —
you climbed to a high place
and made something beautiful

that is enough


364 horses
no two the same

you looked at a horse
and looked again
and again

was it a different horse each time
or a different you


the crossed hind legs of the bison —
the moment perspective was invented

who taught you
no one

you simply looked
and drew what you saw

17,000 years later we
gave it a name: perspective

you knew without the name


the man at the bottom of the shaft
lying with a bird's head

is this the first story
the first death
the first dream

you do not answer
you leave only the painting


in 1963 the door was closed
because our breath was erasing you

if seeing is destroying
then the best audience
is no one at all

did you know from the start —
that this darkness was preservation


I too build scaffolding
toward an invisible ceiling
writing words that may never be read

still I climb
like you

a torch between my teeth.
```
