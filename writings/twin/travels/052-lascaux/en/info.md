# Lascaux Cave

## Discovery

September 12, 1940. Four boys followed a dog and found a hole. Marcel Ravidat, Jacques Marsal, Georges Agniel, Simon Coencas. They were seventeen. When they went inside, enormous animals stood upon the walls.

French archaeologist Henri Breuil conducted the first systematic study.

## Location and Scale

In the Dordogne region, near the village of Montignac, above the Vézère valley. The main chamber is approximately 20 meters wide and 5 meters high. Several steep galleries branch outward.

## Dating

Radiocarbon dating places the paintings at approximately 17,000 years ago. Some scholars believe the images accumulated over hundreds to thousands of years.

## Major Spaces

- **Hall of the Bulls**: Four enormous black aurochs. One measures 5.2 meters in length — the largest animal figure ever found in cave art.
- **Axial Gallery**: Horses, deer, cattle.
- **Passageway**: A connecting section.
- **Nave**: "Crossed Bison" — the crossing of the hind legs demonstrates an understanding of perspective.
- **Chamber of Felines**: Rare depictions of feline animals.
- **Shaft (the Well)**: The "Bird-Headed Man" scene — a human figure collapsed as if dead, a bison, a bird-shaped staff. An enigmatic scene with many competing interpretations.

## Numbers

- Approximately 600 painted images and drawings
- Approximately 1,500 engravings
- Roughly 2,000 figures in total
- Animals: 364 horses, 90 deer, aurochs, bison, felines, bears, rhinoceroses, birds, and one human
- A notable absence: no reindeer. Despite being their primary food source at the time.

## Techniques

- Mineral pigments: various shades of red, black, brown, and yellow
- Pigment applied directly to the wall, engraved into stone, or a combination of both
- Evidence of scaffolding used to reach high surfaces
- Sophisticated understanding of animal anatomy and movement — the animals are depicted "in motion"

## Closure and Preservation

Opened to the public in 1948. Closed in 1963. Artificial lighting and visitors' carbon dioxide caused color degradation and harmful biological growth. Archaeological material was destroyed.

## Replicas and Legacy

- UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979 (as part of the Vézère valley prehistoric sites)
- **Lascaux II** (1983): Partial replica, open to visitors
- **Lascaux IV** (International Centre for Cave Art): 8,000 m² in scale, a complete 1:1 replica, six scenographic spaces, digital experiences. "Prehistory has never been this alive."
