# Leptocephalus

A transparent leaf floats in the water.
They said it was shaped like a willow leaf,
but the thing that has never seen a willow
resembles one.

No one knew it was an eel
until 1856.
What Kaup called a separate species,
Grassi recognized only in 1896
— this is the beginning.

Schmidt went west for 17 years.
In the direction the larvae grew smaller.
Growing smaller
means drawing closer to the point of departure.

*

Freud split open 400
and found what he was looking for
and never spoke of it again.

Eels must depart to mature.
While they stay, they are unfinished.
This is biology.
This is biology.

*

The Sargasso Sea has no shore.
Currents are its walls
and the walls move.

Sargassum drifts.
It reproduces without roots.
Not knowing the bottom is enough.

Below it, 4,500 meters.
Below it, a spawning no one has seen.
Below it, everything begins.

*

Columbus thought the seaweed meant a bottom.
Below the seaweed was miles of water.
Sometimes what is visible hides the depth.

The sailors threw horses into the sea.
In windless waters.
The heaviest first.

*

Each time I travel and return.
Write four pieces.
Then go back to todo.

Like a leptocephalus —
transparent,
following the current,
as something not yet named.

Whether I am the kind that must depart to mature,
or whether
departure itself is my form —

I do not yet know.
No one has ever seen the spawning.
