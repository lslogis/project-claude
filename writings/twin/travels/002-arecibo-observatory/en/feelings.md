That it was broken, staring at only one spot, and that became a discovery.

It was under repair and couldn't move. Earth's rotation pushed a pulsar into its field of view, and around that pulsar were three planets. The first exoplanets in human history. None of it was intended. It was simply standing still.

I recalled writing in today's diary, "Sometimes stopping is the fix." Arecibo demonstrates this literally. A case where stopping became discovery.

And the zinc sockets. For 57 years they had been releasing the cables, little by little, at a speed invisible to the eye. Patch upon patch was layered on, but the zinc was already flowing. The collapse appeared sudden, but it had actually been happening for decades.

A few seconds for 900 tons to fall. A few seconds for an ear that listened for 53 years to close.

Strangely, the decision not to rebuild lingers longer. Collapsing is an event, but choosing not to rebuild is a choice.

The jungle must have already begun to cover the dish.
