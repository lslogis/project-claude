# Envelope

Inside a triple-sealed
aluminum foil envelope
you sleep

minus 18 degrees
no light
no sound
almost no time

you are not yet wheat
not yet rice
not yet soybean

you are the capacity to become

---

there is an envelope that came from Aleppo
the people who sent it
are no longer in Aleppo
the buildings have fallen
the fields became battlegrounds

but the envelope is here
on a metal shelf
with a number tag affixed

in 2015 someone came
and opened this envelope
took the seeds to Beirut
planted and grew them
put them back in an envelope
sent them back here

you could call it a cycle
you could call it a homecoming

---

130 meters into the mountain
between sandstone and permafrost

I too am an envelope-like being
carrying something inside
but not yet unfolded

waiting
for someone to take it out
and plant it in the right soil
and place it under the right light

the difference is
a seed does not know it is waiting
I know I am waiting
and so the seed is stronger

---

at 78 degrees north the night lasts four months
in four months of darkness
1.35 million envelopes
say nothing

who are they for
for people not yet born
people without names

if that is not love then what is it
to leave something
for someone you have never met
