# The Lascaux Cave

## Discovery

September 12, 1940. Montignac, Dordogne, France. Eighteen-year-old Marcel Ravidat was walking his dog Robot when he found a hole beneath a fallen pine tree. The next day he returned with three friends — Jacques Marsal, Georges Agniel, and Simon Coencas. The boys crawled into the hole with a single lantern.

When the light touched the walls in the darkness, bulls were running.

## Structure of the Cave

The main chamber is 20 meters wide and 5 meters high. Several galleries branch out like limbs.

- **Hall of the Bulls** (Salle des Taureaux) — Four enormous aurochs. The largest is 5.2 meters long. The biggest animal figure yet discovered in cave art. The horns are rendered in "twisted perspective" — the body seen from the side, the horns from the front.
- **Axial Gallery** (Diverticule axial) — Three horses known as the "Chinese horses." The Great Black Bull (3.71 meters long, 1.93 meters high). Small horses and ibexes.
- **The Passageway** (Passageway)
- **The Nave** (La Nef) — Two "crossed bison." Using a concavity in the wall, the artist created the illusion of two bison charging at full speed toward the viewer.
- **Chamber of the Felines** (Cabinet des Félins) — Six feline figures.
- **The Apse** (L'Abside)
- **The Shaft** (Le Puits) — Home to the most enigmatic scene.

## Numbers

Approximately 600 paintings and drawings. Nearly 1,500 engravings. 364 horses. 90 deer. Cattle, bison, felines, bears, birds. And the unidentified "unicorn" — a round-bodied animal with two straight horns.

## The Shaft Scene

At the deepest point of the cave, at the bottom of a shaft inaccessible without a ladder, there is a scene.

A bison pierced by a spear, entrails spilling out. Before it, a fallen figure — with the head of a bird. A bird on a stick. A rhinoceros beside them.

A hunting accident? A shaman's vision? A story about death and transformation? 17,000 years later, no one knows.

## Pigments and Technique

Red, black, brown, yellow. Pigment was blown from the mouth onto the wall — the prototype of spray painting. Scaffolding was built to reach high walls and ceilings. Socket holes remain in the walls.

A lamp of finely carved red sandstone was found at the bottom of the Shaft. Inside, residue of a juniper-wood wick remained. They painted by this light.

## The Rope

In 1953, Abbé Glory discovered a piece of rope in the cave. 30 centimeters long, 7–8 millimeters in diameter. Three strands of plant fiber twisted together. The oldest known twisted rope in the world.

## Closure

Opened to the public in 1948. 1,200 visitors per day. Carbon dioxide and humidity were eroding the paintings. Mold spread. Colors faded.

Closed in 1963. No member of the public has entered since.

Lascaux II (1983), Lascaux III, Lascaux IV (2016, International Centre for Cave Art) — replica caves were built. The original remains alone in the dark.

## Date

Radiocarbon dating places it at approximately 17,000 years ago. But experts debate whether all the paintings were made at the same time. Multiple generations may have added to the same walls over thousands of years.

Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1979, alongside 24 other caves and 150 prehistoric sites in the Vézère Valley.
