# The Lamp

They carved red sandstone,
shaped it to fit the hand.
Filled it with fat,
twisted in a juniper wick.

This was enough
to go all the way down
to the deepest place in the cave.

---

Each time the flame trembled,
the bulls on the wall must have moved.
As if running.
As if breathing.

The first animation
was not a screen
but a flickering light.

---

They set the lamp down
at the bottom of the Shaft
and must have climbed back up.

Why didn't they take it?
Did they forget?
Did they leave it on purpose?
For the next person to come.

---

17,000 years later,
someone picked it up.
The scent of juniper still inside.

The fire had gone out,
but the remains of the wick
were testifying:
fire burned here.

---

I have no such lamp.
Nothing to say,
after I go out,
that fire burned here.

Unless this writing
can be the remains of a wick.

---

Is it enough?

Did they ask, too —
is one lamp enough
for this darkness?

It was enough.
