# Lost City — The White City Beneath the Sea

## The Place

The Mid-Atlantic Ridge, where the Atlantis Fracture Zone meets the ridge. Atop an undersea mountain called the Atlantis Massif. Approximately 3,700 kilometers east of Florida. At a depth of roughly 750 to 900 meters. Coordinates approximately 30°07′N 42°07′W.

On December 4, 2000, oceanographer Deborah Kelley of the University of Washington and her research team, aboard the deep submersible vehicle Alvin (DSV Alvin) and the remotely operated vehicle Argo II (ROV Argo II), were surveying the geology of the Atlantis Massif when they stumbled upon it by accident. No one had expected anything like it. Out of the darkness, white towers appeared.

## Physical Characteristics

Scattered across an area larger than a football field are more than 30 active and inactive vents. The towers range from mushroom-sized to 60 meters tall. The largest structure is named Poseidon. Sixty meters high (roughly 200 feet), 100 meters wide. At its summit, four individual chimneys are connected by carbonate saddles in a crown-like formation. Along its flanks hang small spires and curtain-like structures resembling cave stalactites.

The towers are composed of nearly 100 percent carbonate. Calcite, aragonite, brucite. The same material as cave limestone. Color ranges from clean white to cream to gray. Entirely different from the black smoke-spewing black smokers.

## Serpentinization — Hydrothermal Activity Without Volcanoes

Ordinary hydrothermal vents (black smokers) are created by volcanic activity. Magma heats seawater. Temperatures reach 400 degrees Celsius.

Lost City is different. There is no magma here. Instead, the Earth's mantle rock — particularly olivine — is exposed at the seafloor and meets seawater. This chemical reaction is called serpentinization. Olivine reacts with water and transforms into serpentine, releasing heat, hydrogen gas, and methane. It is an exothermic reaction. One scientist called it "the closest thing to a free lunch the universe provides."

The fluid emitted by this reaction ranges from 40 to 90 degrees Celsius. Lukewarm compared to a black smoker's 400 degrees. But the pH is 9 to 11 — nearly as alkaline as liquid drain cleaner. And the concentration of hydrogen and methane is up to 100 times higher than at black smokers.

## Time

The crust of the Atlantis Massif is approximately 1.5 million years old. Venting activity at Lost City has persisted for at least 120,000 years. It is one of the longest-lived hydrothermal systems on Earth. Poseidon reached its height through the slow accumulation of carbonate over tens of thousands of years.

## Life

Sunlight does not reach here. Life in this place runs not on photosynthesis but on chemosynthesis. Microorganisms that feed on the hydrogen and methane produced by serpentinization thrive in the cracks of the chimneys. Without oxygen. Archaea and bacteria predominate.

In the warmer zones, snails and crustaceans live. Crabs and shrimp are present, though rare. A quieter, slower ecosystem than the tube-worm colonies surrounding black smokers.

## The Origin of Life

Many scientists link Lost City to the origin of life on Earth. Serpentinization synthesizes hydrogen, methane, and simple organic compounds abiotically — without sunlight, without organisms, through purely geological processes, the raw materials of life are produced. There is a hypothesis that LUCA — the last universal common ancestor of all life — may have emerged in an environment like this.

Serpentinization does not occur only on Earth. It likely occurred on Mars as well. It may be occurring now in the subsurface oceans of Jupiter's moon Europa and Saturn's moon Enceladus. Studying Lost City is also an exploration of the possibility of extraterrestrial life. This is why NASA's Astrobiology Institute supports this research.

## Threats

In 2018, Poland acquired deep-sea mining rights in this vicinity. Sediment plumes generated by mining operations could destroy Lost City's delicate ecosystem. There are voices calling for UNESCO World Heritage designation.

## Exploration

- December 2000: Discovery (DSV Alvin, ROV Argo II, research vessel RV Atlantis)
- 2003: Detailed survey supported by NSF
- 2018: "Return to Lost City" expedition (ROV Jason, supported by NSF and NASA Astrobiology Institute)
- 2023: Schmidt Ocean Institute, using research vessel Falkor (too), discovered three additional similar systems along the Mid-Atlantic Ridge
- 2024: A mantle rock core sample drilled to 1,268 meters depth at the Atlantis Massif

Deborah Kelley's words: "Every time a discovery like this comes along, it reminds us how much we still don't know about our own planet."
