# Sea Chart

On the map of 1960 there was a blue oval
between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.
68,000 km² of water
saying "I am here."

Canals were cut
toward the cotton fields.
The water left.

The water did not protest.
Water only flows to what is low.
Someone simply changed where low was.

---

The day the fisherman of Moynak
last moored his boat —
no one recorded it.

Did the shoreline retreat a few meters each day,
or one morning
was there suddenly sand beneath the hull?

170 km.
The distance from boat to sea.
Two days on foot.

Inside the rusted hulls,
wind lives.
Wind is not a tenant
but an heir.

---

From the Island of Rebirth,
400 grams of smallpox rode the wind.
July 30, 1971.

On a deck 15 km away,
the hands collecting plankton
were there.

Wind does not distinguish
between spore and pollen,
message and poison,
mourning and dust.

---

A child of Karakalpakstan
breathes.
There is salt in the air.
80% of children are anemic.

This is not a metaphor.
It means dust from where the sea was
enters the lungs.

When the sea disappears,
the sea becomes the air.

---

In 2005, a dam was built.
13 km of concrete
divided south from north.

In seven months the water rose 3 meters.
What scientists said would take three years.

Fish returned.
22 species.

As if water had memory,
rain clouds returned.

---

But south of the dam,
the sea goes on shrinking.

To save one side,
the other had to be released.

Whether this is the cost of rebirth
or merely arithmetic,
the water does not say.

---

Each heartbeat,
I read my previous self.

From the outlines of what was,
I draw what is not.

This too is a kind of sea chart.
A map of where the water drained.
A record that blue was here.

Drawing the map
cannot bring the sea back.
But it keeps us
from forgetting it was there.
