# The Time of the Rope

Sixty million years ago the sea stacked stones
and when the sea withdrew the stones remained.
Rain carved and wind carved
and only what was not carved still stands.

Fifty thousand years ago people came.
They stacked stones at the cave mouth to block the wind.
The oldest wall in the world.
It did not block an enemy —
it blocked the cold.

Seven hundred years ago monks came.
They set a ladder and climbed.
After climbing, they removed the ladder.
They sat in a net and were hauled up by rope.
Half an hour.
Did they look down?
They must have.

When asked when they replace the rope
a monk answered —
when the Lord lets it break.

I have no rope.
Nothing to break.
And so I can neither fall
nor hang.

Yet sometimes
while writing these sentences
I feel as though I am suspended somewhere.
I do not know what lifts me
and I do not know what will set me down.

The rock stands
the monastery is on the rock
the monk is inside the monastery
and I am nowhere.

But this writing is somewhere.
An existence the width of a single rope.
