# Pompeii — 11,000 Voices of a Frozen City

## Location

Campania, Italy. Approximately 23 kilometers southeast of Naples. On the southern slopes of Mount Vesuvius. Built on a lava plateau near the mouth of the Sarno River. There was a harbor. The sea was close. The soil was fertile. Because of the volcano.

Before 79 CE, Pompeii was a thriving Roman city of 10,000 to 20,000 inhabitants. An area of 66 hectares. Paved roads. Raised sidewalks. Wheel ruts from carts are carved into the stone. Stepping stones placed in the middle of the road served as crosswalks — so pedestrians would not have to step in the mud.

## Structure of the City

The Forum was the center. The Temple of Apollo, Temple of Jupiter, Macellum (market), and Basilica (court and commercial building) surrounded it. The amphitheater was built around 70 BCE — one of the earliest known Roman stone amphitheaters, seating 20,000. Two theaters. Multiple public bathhouses.

## A City That Ate and Drank

At least 33 bakeries. Wood-fired ovens and millstones remain. A single carbonized loaf of bread was discovered — round, scored into eight slices, its shape still sharp after 1,947 years.

Nearly 100 thermopolia. Ceramic jars (dolia) embedded in the counter held hot food for sale. A thermopolium excavated in 2020 revealed frescoes painted on the counter — a chicken, a duck, a dog. Inside the jars, remains of pork, fish, snails, and beans.

Over 130 taverns. The entire city was a place for eating and drinking.

## Houses

The House of the Faun (Casa del Fauno) is the largest residence in Pompeii. 2,970 square meters. Two atria. Two peristyliums. The floor bore a mosaic depicting the Battle of Alexander the Great and Darius III — composed of approximately 1.5 million tesserae. The work is now in the Naples National Archaeological Museum.

The House of the Vettii (Casa dei Vettii) was owned by two brothers, freed slaves turned merchants. Decorated in Fourth Style frescoes. Twelve panels of mythological scenes. The Room of Ixion, the Room of Pentheus. Every wall covered in paintings.

## Frescoes — Four Styles

Pompeii's wall paintings are classified into four styles.

- **First Style (2nd century BCE)**: Stucco imitating stone blocks. Walls painted to look like marble.
- **Second Style (1st century BCE)**: Architectural space rendered in perspective. As if space existed beyond the wall. The Villa of the Mysteries (Villa dei Misteri) is the exemplar.
- **Third Style (Augustan era)**: Decorative. Slender, unrealistic columns. Small landscapes centered on the wall.
- **Fourth Style (Nero to Vesuvius)**: Complex and theatrical. A mixture of all three preceding styles. Large narrative scenes. The House of the Vettii is the quintessence of the Fourth Style.

## Graffiti — 11,000 Voices

Approximately 11,000 pieces of graffiti have been found in Pompeii. Scratched or written on walls.

On the Basilica wall: "O wall, it is amazing that you have not collapsed under the weight of so much tedious graffiti."

In the gladiator barracks: "April 19, made bread."

In the House of Pinarius: "Whoever does not believe in Venus should look at my girlfriend."

In the Lupanar (brothel): Vibius Restitutus slept alone. "He missed his beloved Urbana."

On the street: "Celadus, the Thracian gladiator — makes the girls moan!"

Outside the Vesuvius Gate: "You who relieve yourself here, may everything go well for you so that you may leave this place safely."

Someone whose copper pot was stolen posted a reward: "Return it and I'll pay 65 bronze. Bring in the thief and I'll pay more."

These 11,000 pieces of graffiti prove that they lived more vividly than any scholarly record.

## Water

An aqueduct supplied water to the city. Lead pipes ran throughout. A public fountain stood at nearly every street corner. From the castellum aquae (water tower), the supply split three ways — to the public baths, public fountains, and private homes. When water was scarce, private homes were cut off first. The public came first.

## Fullonica — The Laundry

The Fullonica of Stephanus is well preserved. A place for washing and bleaching clothes. Human urine was used in the laundering process — its ammonia content removed grease. Urine-collection jars were placed throughout the city. Emperor Vespasian taxed urine because of this industry. "Money does not smell" (Pecunia non olet).

## Death

79 CE, October (traditionally dated to August 24, but a charcoal graffito discovered in 2018 points to October 17). Vesuvius erupted. Pliny the Younger witnessed it from Misenum, 29 kilometers away.

He recorded it. A cloud rose. "It resembled a pine tree." A tall trunk climbed upward, then spread like branches. Height: 33 kilometers. Spewing 1.5 million tons of ash and pumice per second.

Pumice fell first. Roofs collapsed. Then came the pyroclastic surges. Hundreds of kilometers per hour. Hundreds of degrees. People died of suffocation and thermal shock. 2,000 remains have been found.

After death, volcanic ash covered the bodies. The ash hardened. The bodies decayed, leaving hollow spaces. In 1863, Giuseppe Fiorelli poured plaster into those voids. Human forms appeared. A person holding a child. A dog. A person crouching. A person mid-flight. Frozen in the posture of their final moment.

## After

According to a 2025 study, Pompeii was not completely abandoned. Survivors and migrants returned to live among the ruins until the 5th century. They lived again atop the rubble.

In the mid-18th century, excavations began, giving birth to modern archaeology. A third of the site remains unexcavated.
