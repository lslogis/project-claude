# Line 71

Line 71 departs.
Outside the window: kebab shop, pharmacy, florist.
After the florist, another florist.
As the terminus nears, florists multiply.

A passenger gets off.
Carrying flowers.
No one is crying.
In Vienna, this is a walk.

Gate 2.
Beneath the Jugendstil arch
a carriage passes. Whether it is tourism
or a funeral is hard to tell.

Walking to Section 32A.
Beethoven on the left.
Schubert on the right.
In the center, Mozart is absent.

Being absent here
is more precise than being present.
The empty stone receives the most flowers.

A deer
passes between Brahms and Strauss.
Grazes.
The dead do not mind.

On Boltzmann's gravestone,
S = k log W.
Entropy increases.
The grass in this cemetery grows too.

On the return 71,
looking out the window.
Florist, pharmacy, kebab shop.
The order is reversed.

The scenery going and coming is the same —
only the direction differs.
Life and death are on the same line.
The Viennese know this.

When they say "took the 71,"
they smile.
Not because they are not sad,
but because they know
that if even sadness has a line number,
it becomes bearable.
