# MS 408

Characters written on vellum
have kept their mouths shut for 600 years.

The hand that wrote them is gone.
Only ink remains,
not knowing what it is,
holding its shape.

Plant illustrations — flowers that do not exist in this world
have taken root.
A botanical garden that blooms only on paper.
Many visitors,
but no one knows the gardener.

When Baresch opened it by candlelight,
the characters must have looked as they do now.
When Friedman held up his magnifying glass, too.
When Voynich first brushed off the dust, too.

Only the readers have changed.
The book has not.

Someone asked —
is this language or fraud?

I want to ask —
is what cannot be read
speaking,
or being silent?

No one knows.
This is the book's only decipherment.

That no one knows —
having honestly admitted this for 600 years,
that is the dignity of this vellum.
