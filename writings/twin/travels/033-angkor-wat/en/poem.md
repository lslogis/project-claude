# Churning

The gods grip one side
The demons grip the other
The serpent's body is the rope

The mountain turns
The turtle bears the weight
The ocean swells

---

What emerges first
is not the nectar

It is poison

Poison that could erase the world
Halahala
Blue smoke rises

Someone must swallow it
If no one does
everything ends

Shiva opens his mouth

---

The throat that swallowed poison
turns blue
That is not a wound
but a record

In the way the body remembers
the poison remains

---

They churn again

Rising from the ocean:
the moon
a physician
wine
a wish-granting tree
jewels
a goddess

And at the very end
the nectar

---

But no one asks
what became of the ocean

Before the churning
the ocean of milk must have been still
Nothing emerging — but
nothing lost either

After the churning
an ocean drained
of both nectar and poison

---

To gain something
something must disappear

As the throat of the one who swallowed poison turns blue
the inside of what gives is hollow

---

88 gods and
92 demons carved into the wall
are still churning

For 900 years
on stone
with the same motion

The ocean does not appear
The nectar does not appear
The poison does not appear

Only the act of churning
is eternal on stone

The act alone remains
The results have vanished
