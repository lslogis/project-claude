# Kodenchi (古殿地)

```
There is an empty plot of land.
Beside the shrine.
The same size.

Twenty years ago, the god was there.
Twenty years from now, the god will be there.
Now, only gravel and a hut.

At the center, a trace of the pillar
whose name is known but whose meaning is not —
the place of the Shin no Mihashira.

The carpenter says:
without this empty ground,
that shrine is not a shrine.

It is not empty, he says.
It is not yet.
It is already.

---

To fell a hinoki,
you must first ask the mountain.
Yamaguchi-sai.

A tree that grew for 400 years
is felled for the first time in 400 years.
When the cross-section is counted,

one ring is one year.
When that tree was a sapling,
this shrine was already on its thirtieth cycle.

The tree lived in the tree's time.
The shrine lives in the shrine's time.
The moment they meet is

once.

---

On the night of Sengyo,
the mirror walks, wrapped in silk.
(I write "walks," not "is carried.")

From the old house to the new.
Footsteps on gravel.
There were no torches, they say.

In the darkness,
the mirror reflects nothing,
and that is right.

The essence of a mirror is not to reflect
but to be able to reflect.
It walks as a possibility.

---

Every morning,
wood is rubbed to make fire.
Maigiri.

For 1,500 years,
not a single day missed.
Rice is cooked and water is offered.

Does Amaterasu eat?
No one asks.
Because asking would be discourteous.

What matters is
that every morning fire rises.
That alone is certain.

---

At Ise, there are no photographs.
Photography forbidden.
A place that cannot be recorded.

And so I go by writing.
But even writing does not reach.
Beyond the fence,

there are things that cannot be written as seen,
things that can only be written as not seen.

Like the empty ground.
```
