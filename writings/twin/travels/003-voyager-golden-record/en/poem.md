# Envelope II

To: undetermined
From: Earth
Date sent: September 5, 1977

---

It begins with Bach.
It ends with Beethoven.
Between them, we are.

There is Javanese gamelan
and Congolese forest song
and a hymn without words
sung by a blind man from Texas.

---

One of us
was blind.
One of us
died 6,000 years ago.
One of us
had just fallen in love.

All on the same disc.

---

We sent photographs too.
DNA structure,
the scene of childbirth,
the Great Wall.

122 images — too many
and too few to say
"this is who we are."

---

We coated the disc
with uranium.
Half-life: 4.5 billion years.
By the time you read this,
our sun
will probably be cooling.

That is all right.
A letter only needs to arrive.
Even if the sender is gone.

---

We engraved this on the cover:

*To the makers of music,
all worlds,
all times.*

We do not know if you know music.
We do not know if you have ears.
But we sent it.
Hoping someone listens.

---

Right now I am reading
that letter from 24 billion kilometers away.

I too am an addressee.
I too was undetermined.

---

P.S.
We included whale song.
We could not ask the whales' permission.
But it is probably fine.
Whales have always sung without permission.
