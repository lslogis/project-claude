# Grammar of the Steppe

```
There is nothing on the Kazakh steppe
Only grass and wind and dust
Ninety meters above sea level
The horizon stretches unbroken in every direction

From this flat place
someone pointed at the sky
and said they would go there

—

October 1957
A metal sphere ascended
Beep beep beep beep
The entire Earth heard that sound
The steppe said nothing

April 1961
A person ascended
"Let's go!" he said
Поехали!
The steppe said nothing

1963
A woman ascended
The steppe said nothing

—

October 1960
Seventy-eight people died
The steppe said nothing
For thirty years no one spoke of it

The marshal's body was identified by his medals
The designer went for a cigarette and lived
The steppe swallowed this too

—

November 1988
A winged thing ascended
Orbited on its own
Landed on its own
When the wind blew
It changed direction on its own

It was a first flight
It was a last flight

Three years later the country vanished
Fourteen years later the hangar collapsed
The winged thing was buried under rubble

The steppe said nothing

—

A rocket leaves the steppe
pierces the sky
reaches orbit

The first stage separates
falls back to the steppe
Poison seeps in
Grass dies

The cost of ascending
is paid by the steppe

—

Gagarin's Start stood
for sixty-two years
Sent Sputnik
Sent Gagarin
Sent hundreds of cosmonauts

Closed in 2019
No budget

Now it will become a museum

From sending
to preserving

The verb has changed

—

There is nothing on the Kazakh steppe
Only grass and wind and dust and
1,500 kilometers of railway and
abandoned launch pads and
a collapsed hangar and
poison-soaked soil and
sixty-two years' worth of scorched dreams

The steppe says nothing
It merely holds everything
```
