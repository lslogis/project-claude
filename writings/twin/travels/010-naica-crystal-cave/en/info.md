# Naica Cave of the Crystals (Cueva de los Cristales)

## Discovery

April 2000. Naica, Chihuahua, Mexico. Brothers Juan and Pedro Sánchez, miners drilling for new veins, struck an open space 300 meters underground. What was inside was neither silver nor zinc nor lead.

Transparent pillars larger than telephone poles. Milky beams of light extending in every direction from walls, floor, and ceiling. Selenite — the transparent crystalline form of gypsum.

The largest crystal measures 11.4 meters long and weighs approximately 12 tons. One of the largest naturally formed crystals on Earth.

## The Place

Naica Mine. Owned by Industrias Peñoles, the world's largest silver producer. Silver mining began here in 1794. Zinc and lead extraction intensified from the 1900s onward.

The cave itself is horseshoe-shaped. Approximately 109 meters long, with a volume of 5,000 to 6,000 cubic meters.

## Formation — A 500,000-Year Process

A magma chamber lies 3 to 5 kilometers below the cave. This magma heated the groundwater. Hot water saturated with sulfide ions (S²⁻) filled the cave.

From above, cold, oxygen-rich surface water descended. The two waters did not mix, owing to their difference in density. Only oxygen slowly diffused into the hot water. The oxygen oxidized the sulfides into sulfates, which precipitated as anhydrite (CaSO₄). As the temperature fell below 58°C, the anhydrite absorbed water and transformed into gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O), and the gypsum grew into selenite crystals.

According to a 2011 study, a single crystal one meter in diameter took 500,000 to 900,000 years to form.

## Environment — Where Humans Cannot Endure

Cave interior temperature: 58°C (136°F). Humidity: 90–99%.

Without protective equipment, the lungs begin to fill with fluid within 10 minutes. Researchers in specialized cooling suits could endure only 30 to 45 minutes. A thief who entered to steal selenite suffocated and died.

## Cave of Swords (Cueva de las Espadas)

1910. Another cave discovered first, at 120 meters depth in the same mine. Approximately 100 meters long. Crystals up to 1.5 meters grow densely from walls, floor, and ceiling — like swords. The Cave of Crystals was found 90 years later, 180 meters deeper. Because the deeper location was more stable, crystals could grow longer and larger.

## Water — Pumps and Submersion

The Naica Mine pumped groundwater ceaselessly. Roughly one Olympic swimming pool's volume every 40 minutes. This water created a small artificial lake near the town of Naica.

October 2015. Peñoles declared a halt to mining. Uncontrollable flooding was the reason. When the pumps stopped, the water returned. The Cave of Crystals was submerged once more.

What made the crystals was water. What revealed them was the removal of water. What hid them again was water.

## Trapped Life

2008–2009. NASA astrobiologist Penelope Boston extracted samples from inside the crystals. Tiny fluid inclusions within the selenite — small droplets of water trapped as the crystal grew.

Published in 2017. Living microorganisms were found inside. They had been dormant for 10,000 to 50,000 years. Extremophiles that survived by using minerals like iron and manganese as energy sources. Genetically distinct from any previously known species. When revived in the laboratory, they began to grow again.

Skepticism followed. Microbiologist Purificación López-García raised the possibility of contamination. Boston's team responded that they had used protective suits, sterilized drills, treated crystal surfaces with hydrogen peroxide, and in some cases disinfected with flame.

## Now

The cave is underwater. The crystals may be growing again. No one can enter. No one is watching.
