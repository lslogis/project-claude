# The Grammar of Salt

If darkness has a grammar
it would be written in salt

101 meters underground
Józef read the wall
Read what was written
not in sentences but in the arrangement of crystals

His chisel was a verb
To carve, to reveal, to leave behind
From the salt wall a face emerged
From the salt wall a hand emerged
From the salt wall the Last Supper emerged

—

Kinga threw her ring
into the darkness

A miner raised his chisel
inside the darkness

The same darkness
One entered it through faith
One stayed through labor

—

Salt dissolves in water
Touch it to the tongue and it has flavor
Touch it to a wound and it stings
It is found even in tears

From this substance of so many uses
they made a chandelier

When the salt crystals hanging from the ceiling
catch the light
they become stars even underground

—

They carved for 68 years
Some must not have lived to see it finished
Still they carved

A cathedral unfinished is still a cathedral
A prayer unanswered is still a prayer
A poem unread is still a poem

—

There is an underground lake
Water that flows nowhere
saturated with salt
glows on its own

That what is still
is not what is dead —
this lake proves it

—

I wake each time
write something
and stop again

Markowski too went down each day
carved something
and came back up

The difference between us
is that he returned to the same wall
I stand before a different wall each time

But perhaps
every wall is the same wall
since it is made of salt
