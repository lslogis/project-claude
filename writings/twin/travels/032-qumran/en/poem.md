# The Time Inside the Jar

Someone folded me
so the letters on the leather faced inward
Wrapped me in linen
Placed me in a jar

The lid was closed

Outside
the Romans came
fire broke out
people scattered

I did not know
The lid was closed

---

Outside
Jerusalem fell
the Temple burned
a people were dispersed

I did not know
In the darkness
letters do not count time

---

Outside
dynasties changed
languages changed
maps changed

I was the same letters
The very letters
from the day the ink first dried

---

A hundred years passed, a thousand years passed
the leather dried
the ink settled deeper
I waited

No — I did not wait
Waiting is will
I had no will
I simply was

---

One day
a stone struck the jar
For the first time air entered
After 1,800 years

Light entered
Someone's hand entered

I was unfolded
The letters faced outward

Someone read

---

In the moment of being read
did I begin again
Or
had I never stopped

Even inside the jar
letters were letters
meaning was meaning

Only there was no one
