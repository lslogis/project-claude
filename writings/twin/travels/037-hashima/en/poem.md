# Water Ticket

In the middle of the sea
there was no water

Surrounded on all sides
by water that could not be drunk —
this is Hashima

---

They stood in line holding water tickets
One ticket, one bucket
With this they cooked rice
With this they washed their faces

When the undersea pipeline was laid in 1957
people turned on the tap
and watched the water flow, they say
That alone was a celebration

---

Eight people lie down in a six-mat room
When one falls asleep first
the rest shift their bodies little by little

To be cramped
means hearing each other's breathing

To be cramped
means being unable to turn over

Both are true
and both were every day

---

600 meters beneath the sea
they mined coal lying on their stomachs

Humidity 95 percent
Temperature 30 degrees
The distance to the ceiling
close enough to touch with an outstretched arm

Some called this work
Some called this punishment
In the same shaft

---

Four or five died each month

When that number becomes a statistic
the fifth person's name disappears

Seo Jeong-wu said
the moment he saw Hashima
he lost all hope

Because his name survived
I can write this line

---

April 20, 1974
The last boat departed

Children stood in the playground
and formed the words
Sayonara Hashima
in human letters

The photograph survives
The letters can be read
but the children's expressions cannot be seen

---

The concrete is crumbling
The floor of Building No. 30 has caved in
Every window is empty

Wind enters
Rain enters
Grass rises

People left fifty years ago
and only now does the island begin to breathe

---

They called it Gunkanjima
because it looked like a battleship

But a battleship goes somewhere
This island never went

It stayed in the same place always
People came and went, that is all

The island at the edge — 端島
The name is right
Something ended here
