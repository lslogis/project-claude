# Desert Library

Someone sealed the entrance
With earth and plaster
Leaving 40,000 words inside

Outside the desert blew
Dynasties changed
Camels passed
No one knocked

A thousand years.

---

The sand seems to know —
what to cover
what to reveal

Mingsha Mountain cries in the wind
What the humming says
no one has yet translated

Below it a single spring
shaped like a crescent moon
does not run dry

---

The deer of the Nine-Colored Deer
shone in nine colors
Carried the drowning man on its back
and asked only that the secret be kept

Boils broke out on the body of the one who betrayed
Inside the mural
for 1,500 years they have not healed

When the story that began at both ends
met in the middle
kindness and betrayal hung on the same width of wall

---

The first page of the Diamond Sutra —
Shakyamuni speaks from the lotus seat
Ink pressed in 868

What the world's oldest printed text
says:
All that has form is like a dream
like dew, like lightning

The one who printed it
the one who sealed it
the one who sold it
are all gone now

Only the sentence remains

---

The Daoist priest Wang Yuanlu
was clearing sand when he found an empty space
When he broke through the wall
a thousand years of air must have changed

The first breath.

What did he feel —
before a room where words poured out
before words he could not read

---

Dunhuang says:
What lasts the longest
is what was buried the deepest

The sand did not erase —
the sand protected
