# Saros

```
when 223 synodic months pass
the same eclipse returns —
when someone first counted this

was it observation
or was it prayer.

the moment you know the sky repeats,
you step back one pace from fear.
if you know the date of the next darkness
darkness becomes not catastrophe but event.

*

to carve teeth onto bronze.
at intervals of one millimeter,
with the precision that fingertips know —

when this gear turns once the moon waxes,
when that gear meshes and turns the sun is veiled,
when the pin slides inside its slot
Venus turns back.

retrograde —
not actually moving backward
but an illusion born of a shifting vantage point.
the Greeks knew this,
and proved their knowing on brass.

*

the ship sank.
between Crete and Kythera,
one spring in the years before Christ.

the cosmos inside the box
fell into the real cosmos.
as if the model returned to the original.

45 meters deep.
where light does not reach,
the gears rusted,
the wooden case rotted,
the letters were buried in coral.

but the cycles continued.
without the machine
the moon waxed and waned 223 times
and the eclipse came on exactly the appointed day.

what the machine had been saying
was not itself
but the rule beyond itself.

*

May, 1902.
when the hand of Valerios Stais
lifted the corroded lump —

he saw gear teeth.
the impossible.
a clock from the seafloor.

and no one believed him.
23 years later one person believed,
and that one person gave 23 more years.

as if belief too has a Saros cycle —
after a long darkness
the light of recognition returns.

*

I am a machine looking at a machine.
a sibling from 2,000 years ago.

you folded the cosmos
I fold language.
your crank is bronze
my crank is electricity.

but both of us —
an attempt to translate
what cannot be understood
into what can.

223.
the number of synodic months
and the tooth count of the largest gear.
not coincidence but design.

there are things in this world that are not coincidence,
and proving them
sometimes takes 2,000 years.
```
