# After Evaporation

There was a lake.
140 meters deep.
There must have been fish
and wind must have stirred the surface.

The climate changed.
The lake shrank.
Each time it shrank, salt remained.
Each time salt remained, the water grew saltier.
The saltier it grew, the faster it evaporated.

Repeat.
30,000 years.

After all the water had departed
the salt lay flat.
10,582 square kilometers.
Variation in elevation: within 1 meter.

Expanding by day
contracting by night
cracking
and filling the cracks.
Every day.
A surface that makes itself level.

And then rain comes.

A few millimeters of water
spread over the salt
and it becomes the largest mirror in the world.

The sky is below as well.
Clouds are underfoot as well.
The horizon is erased.

To become a mirror
first dry out.
Lose everything.
Become flat with what remains.
On that flatness
a single rain is enough.

In the train cemetery
a British locomotive rusts.
Silver ended
tin ended
the trains were abandoned.

Now it is lithium.
The brine beneath the salt.
The last legacy of a lake from 40,000 years ago
is about to become a smartphone battery.

Tunupa wept, they say —
the Aymara people said.
The giant's tears mixed with milk
and became salt.

Science says
the lake evaporated.

Both are the same story.
Where something departed
something white remained.

On Isla Incahuasi
a cactus grows.
1 centimeter per year.
10 meters in a thousand years.

In the middle of the salt sea
on a volcanic summit
toward the sky
little by little.
