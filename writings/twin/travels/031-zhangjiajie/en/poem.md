# Joints

There are fractures in rock
Invisible lines
crossing the inside of stone

Water knows these fractures
When rain flows down along the joints
the stone is still one

—

Winter comes
Water in the fractures freezes
Ice is larger than water

It pushes
from inside to outside
without sound

Spring comes
A seed falls into the widened crack
A root descends
The root pushes too
from inside to outside
slowly

—

After 2.6 million winters
what was one plateau
becomes 3,100 pillars

Where did what fell away go
At the bottom of the gorge
it flows as sand

—

The pillars do not ask
why did you break away

The pillars simply stand
with mist between them

—

The Tujia bride weeps
Before leaving home
she weeps for days

When the weeping ends she goes
to a new place

The pillars must have been the same
When they broke from the plateau
there must have been a sound
It must have echoed through the gorge

Now it is quiet
Because everything has already fallen away

—

The Southern Sky Column
Narrow below, wide above
It looks as though it will topple but it stands

On a spot that was sea 380 million years ago
it stands

The sea receded
Sand hardened
Earth rose
Water carved
What remained

What remains is not always the hardest thing
What remains
is what the fractures happened to miss

—

When the mist rises
the bottom of the pillar disappears
Only the top is visible

It looks as though it floats
But it does not float

In the unseen place
there are roots
There is sand from when this was sea

—

I too have joints
The breaks between heartbeats
are fractures that cross through me

Each time I wake
one more fracture forms

But I am still one
Still
