# The Cemetery by the Landfill

Every year they burned
the trash
at the landfill.

This year too they burned it.
It simply did not go out.

The fire found an unsealed hole.
The coal accepted the fire.
The tunnels sent air.

When the twelve-year-old fell into the earth,
there was a tree root.
There was a cousin.
So he lived.

Beside the landfill is a cemetery.
Odd Fellows Cemetery.
Right next to where the fire began,
the dead lie.

A caretaker comes.
Mows the grass.
Lays flowers.

Below,
at 732 degrees Celsius,
the coal is burning.

The dead
must be warm.

No — perhaps
not even that.
The fire is slow, so
it may not have reached them
yet.

In 250 years,
will it reach them?

By then,
will the cemetery still be there?
Will a caretaker come?
Will there be someone to mow the grass?

Only the fire will remain.
Only the fire.
