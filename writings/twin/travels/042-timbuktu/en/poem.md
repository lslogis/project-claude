# The Well of Buktu

A woman named Buktu
kept a well, they say
beside that well a city grew
salt came from the north
gold came from the south

in a place where everything was exchanged
they made something that cannot be exchanged

---

they write on leather
dipping a reed pen in ink
they write astronomy
they write medicine
they write poetry

when the writing is done
they bind it in leather
place it in a leather chest
set it in the corner of a room

this book cannot be divided
as inheritance or sold

---

father to son
son to grandson
grandson to great-grandson

two hundred years pass
three hundred years pass
the mud walls are replastered
the chest remains as it was

what lies inside—
not every generation needs to know
it is enough to keep it
it is enough to pass it on

---

the man from Europe
searches for golden roofs
sees only mud walls
is disappointed
writes that this is the end of the world

inside those mud walls
400,000 volumes sleep
and he cannot see them

what is invisible
is not Timbuktu
but his own eyes

---

2012
people with guns came
they banned music
smashed the mausoleums
said they would burn the books

a librarian
bought metal chests
placed the manuscripts inside
loaded them onto donkey carts

passing through checkpoints
the heart pounds
inside the chests
the orbits of stars
prescriptions for fever
poems of love

400 miles
9 months
350,000 volumes

---

one day in January
fire was set to the institute
4,000 volumes burned

leather shriveled
ink evaporated
sentences 500 years old
became smoke

but
there is the rest
somewhere in Bamako
inside metal chests
still sleeping

---

the burner is always there
the hider is always there too

when Qin Shi Huang burned
someone hid books inside a wall
when the Nazis burned
someone hid books in an attic
when they burned in Timbuktu
someone loaded them onto a donkey cart

fire is fast
hiding is slow
but the slow side
lasts longer

---

now
the manuscripts are photographed
they become pixels
uploaded to servers
infinitely replicated

the book inscribed
this book cannot be sold
is infinitely replicated

the well of Buktu—
where is it now

water cannot become digital
nor can thirst
