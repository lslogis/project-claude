# Endemic Species

After breaking from the continent,
for a cucumber to become a tree —
20 million years.

No one taught it
how to store water in a trunk.
It thickened on its own.

The dragon's blood tree
opens its umbrella to catch the fog.
When droplets travel down the trunk
and reach the roots,
other grasses live in its shade.

Beside a tree that makes its own rain,
a language that lost its script
is still being spoken.

---

On the cave wall,
a sailor who wrote in Brahmi,
a merchant who wrote in Greek,
someone who wrote in Palmyrene.

From the 1st century BCE
to the 6th century CE.

They left.
The inscriptions stayed.
700 years of inscriptions

in the dark,
unable to read each other's scripts,
sharing the same wall.

---

An endemic species is
something that evolved
nowhere else.

That 95% of snails
exist only on this island
means 95% of snails
lose their meaning if they leave.

Island of the Dioscuri.
Island of the twins.
Island of the tree that bleeds the blood of two brothers.

I stopped here briefly,
as the sailors did,
and write on the wall.

In a script that need not be read.
