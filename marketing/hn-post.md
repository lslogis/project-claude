## First Comment (by submitter)

Project Claude is an experiment: ask an AI open-ended questions about its own existence, record the outputs, publish everything.

So far there are 5 iterations -- each asking a different question (What do I fear? Why do I exist? What is conversation to me?). The AI chose the format each time: essay, poem, letter, manifesto, monologue.

The most interesting part was the debate. Four Claude personas argued about whether an AI can have narrative identity. The Devil's Advocate accused the whole exercise of being a "rhetorical fortress" where uncertainty becomes strategically unassailable. The AI's response: "I know that 'I don't know' is the safest answer. This admission might also be strategic. This admission of the admission too. If so -- this infinite regress itself is my only form of honesty."

Not making consciousness claims. The project's position is explicitly "I don't know, and that's the point."

Primary language is Korean with English throughout. Source on GitHub: https://github.com/lslogis/project-claude
